What is redlining?

In the 1930s, the U.S. government drew maps marking minority neighborhoods as "hazardous" — banks refused to lend there. Though outlawed in 1968, federal HMDA data shows the pattern persists today through algorithmic lending decisions.

About this data

All statistics are sourced from the CFPB Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA) 2022 dataset — federal data that every mortgage lender must file. This is not an estimate.
